# Time Entry

At Nymbl, accurate time entry is not just a best practice—it’s a **cornerstone of our operations and a standard part of working in a consulting company**. Time entry is integral for **precise billing, resource allocation, and serves as a key component of project management,** helping us deliver value to our clients while maintaining the efficiency and integrity of our projects.

Consulting companies rely on meticulous time tracking to monitor workloads, identify inefficiencies, and balance resources effectively. By logging your time accurately, you help Nymbl forecast resource needs, optimize utilization, and monitor project budgets to optimize billing and prevent budget overages.

Time is one of our most valuable resources. Adhering to our time entry guidelines ensures that we manage it wisely. This practice promotes transparency, accountability, and proactive planning, enabling us to meet both current and future client needs. Your commitment to detailed and timely entries supports Nymbl’s mission to deliver high-quality results and keeps our projects running smoothly.

## Time Entry Best Practices

Time entry is an essential component of accurate record-keeping and billing. Employees must log hours worked, as well as PTO, sick leave, and holiday leave, in the NOS system.

Daily and Weekly Logging:

* Time entries should ideally be recorded at the end of each workday to ensure accuracy.  
* If daily logging is not feasible, time entries must be completed by the end of each week at the latest.  
* Weekly submissions are due by 1pm eastern of business the following Monday.

Monthly Submission Deadline:

* All time entries for the month must be submitted and signed off by the last day of the month to facilitate timely invoicing.

Time Tracking Guidelines:

* Use sound judgment when tracking time, and round entries to the nearest 15-minute increment.  
* All employees are required to review and sign off on their weekly time entries as confirmation of submission.  
* Full-time employees who do not meet 40 hours per week must include a note explaining the discrepancy.  
* Employees that are concerned they won’t meet their 40 hours per week should reach out to their manager for assistance.

Notifications and Accountability:

* Notifications for missing or late time entries will be sent to the employee and their manager.  
* Consistently late submissions may impact performance reviews and eligibility for variable compensation (VC) bonuses.

Variable Compensation Requirements:

* Employees are expected to log a minimum of 1,824 hours annually to qualify for variable compensation bonuses.  
* Individuals who do not meet the minimum requirement will not be eligible to receive VC bonuses.

Adhering to these best practices ensures accurate reporting, supports operational efficiency, and protects employees' eligibility for performance-based rewards.

# Responsible Use of AI

Nymbl supports the responsible use of artificial intelligence (AI) to streamline processes, reduce manual effort, and enable employees to deliver meaningful insights and high-quality solutions to our clients. This policy establishes clear expectations for AI use while protecting company and client data, ensuring human oversight, and supporting compliance with security and confidentiality standards, including SOC 2\.

This policy applies to all employees and contractors who use AI tools for company or client-related work, including generative AI, application-generation tools, and AI-assisted analytics.

## Approved AI Tools & Vendor Controls

Employees may use **company-approved AI platforms only** (e.g., ChatGPT, Gemini, approved app-generation tools). AI tools must be reviewed and approved prior to use to ensure appropriate security, data handling, and vendor risk controls consistent with SOC 2 requirements. Use of unapproved AI tools for company or client work is prohibited.

## Acceptable Use

AI tools may be used to:

* Draft, summarize, or analyze content  
* Support research, ideation, and problem-solving  
* Assist with code generation and application development  
* Automate repetitive or low-risk tasks

AI should be used as a **productivity and support tool**, not as a replacement for professional judgment, required approvals, or established controls.

## Human Review, Accuracy & Accountability

* **All AI-generated outputs must be reviewed, validated, and approved by a human** before being shared internally or externally.  
* Employees remain fully accountable for the accuracy, security, quality, and compliance of AI-assisted work.  
* AI-generated content must meet the same standards as work produced without AI.

## Data Protection, Confidentiality & Access Controls

To comply with SOC 2 security and confidentiality principles:

* Employees may **not input confidential, proprietary, client, personal, or sensitive data** into AI tools unless explicitly approved  
* Examples may include, but are not limited to: Credentials, source code repositories, customer data, and non-public company information may not be submitted to AI systems without authorization

When in doubt, please reach out to the IT channel for guidance.

## Prohibited Uses

Employees may not:

* Use AI to bypass security controls, monitoring, or approval processes  
* Rely on AI for legal, HR, financial, or compliance advice without appropriate review and authorization  
* Represent AI-generated work as fully autonomous or unreviewed  
* Use AI in ways that violate company policies, client contracts, or applicable laws

## Intellectual Property & Client Deliverables

* AI-generated content may not be unique or protected by copyright; employees are responsible for ensuring proper use and attribution where required  
* Client-facing deliverables must comply with contractual obligations and security requirements  
* Disclosure of AI use to clients must occur when required by contract, law, or company policy

## Monitoring & Reporting

AI usage may be subject to monitoring and audit to ensure compliance with security, confidentiality, and SOC 2 controls. Employees are expected to promptly report any suspected misuse, security incidents, or data exposure involving AI tools to the IT and/or People Operations Teams.

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination, in accordance with company policy.

## Long-Term Medical Leave

## Federal Family Medical Leave 

### Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA) \- What’s “Given” per federal law:

The FMLA mandates that eligible employees are entitled to up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave for specified family and medical reasons. Eligibility criteria include:

* **Employee Tenure:** The employee must have worked for the employer for at least 12 months.  
* **Hours Worked:** The employee must have completed at least 1,250 hours of service in the 12 months preceding the leave.  
* For remote employees, eligibility is determined based on the location to which they report or from which their work is assigned.

### Parental Leave and Utilization Policy

To ensure fairness and clarity, please refer to the information below on Parental Leave and its impact on Utilization and Bonus calculations.

#### Parental Leave and Utilization Metrics

1. Total and Weighted Billable Utilization  
   * Employees on approved Parental Leave (e.g., Maternity or Paternity Leave) will have their utilization capacity reduced to 0 for the duration of their leave.  
   * This ensures that neither the individual’s nor their manager’s utilization metrics are negatively impacted.  
2. Bonus Proration  
   * Employees on Parental Leave will receive their salary during the leave period up to the agreed limits  
   * Bonus eligibility will be prorated based on the portion of the year worked.  
   * For example, an employee who takes three months of Parental Leave would be eligible for up to 75% of their bonus, assuming performance targets are met.

* This policy applies only to Parental Leave and does not extend to other forms of PTO.  
* Employees should coordinate with their managers, Resource Management, and HR to ensure proper documentation of their leave period.

#### Pregnancy & Childbirth Accommodation Policy

Nymbl is committed to fostering an inclusive and supportive work environment for all employees, including those who are pregnant, have recently given birth, or are experiencing related medical conditions. In compliance with the Pregnant Workers Fairness Act (PWFA) and applicable state and local laws, Nymbl will provide reasonable accommodations to qualified employees, unless doing so would impose an undue hardship on business operations.

##### Eligibility

This policy applies to all employees: full-time, part-time, temporary, and remote, who are limited in their ability to perform their job duties due to pregnancy, childbirth, or related medical conditions.

##### Requesting an Accommodation

Employees who wish to request a pregnancy or childbirth-related accommodation should:

1. Submit a written request to the People Operations (HR) team as soon as possible.  
   1. Requests should include a brief description of the accommodation being sought and, if applicable, supporting documentation (e.g., a note from a healthcare provider).  
2. The People Operations team will engage in an interactive process with the employee to discuss their needs and identify an appropriate, effective accommodation.  
3. All requests and related medical information will be kept confidential and shared only as needed to arrange the accommodation.

Examples of Reasonable Accommodations:

The Company recognizes that the needs of pregnant employees and new parents may vary, particularly in a remote work environment. Examples of reasonable accommodations may include, but are not limited to:

* Flexible scheduling or adjusted work hours to accommodate medical appointments, rest, or fatigue.  
* Modified attendance or reduced workload during recovery or treatment periods.  
* Ergonomic adjustments for home office setups (e.g., supportive chair, footrest, adjustable monitor height) to be used in conjunction with the Work From Home Stipend  
* Time off or flexibility for pregnancy-related medical needs, postpartum recovery, or lactation.  
* Modified communication expectations (e.g., allowing for video-off meetings or reduced camera time).  
* Temporary reassignment or delay of non-essential deadlines or travel.

Each accommodation request will be evaluated individually, based on the nature of the job, the employee’s specific needs, and the operational requirements of their project work.

Nymbl is not required to provide an accommodation that would cause undue hardship, meaning a significant difficulty or expense in relation to our size, resources, or operational needs. If an accommodation is deemed an undue hardship, the People Ops Team will work with the employee to explore alternative options that meet both the employee’s needs and business requirements.

Nymbl strictly prohibits retaliation or discrimination against any employee who requests an accommodation, participates in the interactive process, or exercises rights under the PWFA or related laws.

Employees are encouraged to speak openly and promptly with the People Ops Team to ensure their needs are supported in a respectful, confidential, and compliant manner.

# Anti-Harassment & Discrimination

We are committed to providing a work environment that provides our entire workforce with equality, respect, dignity, and safety. * the conduct is severe or pervasive enough to create a work environment that a reasonable person would consider intimidating, hostile, or abusive.

## Anti-Sexual Harassment

Sexual harassment is a form of workplace discrimination and will not be tolerated. ## What is "Sexual Harassment"?

Sexual harassment is a form of sex discrimination and is unlawful under federal, state, and (where applicable) local law. Sexual harassment is unwelcome conduct of a sexual nature which makes a person feel offended, humiliated and/or intimidated. It includes situations where a person is asked to engage in sexual activity as a condition of that person’s employment, as well as situations which create an environment which is hostile, intimidating or humiliating for the recipient.

* Such conduct has the purpose or effect of unreasonably interfering with an individual’s work performance or creating an intimidating, hostile or offensive work environment, even if the reporting individual is not the intended target of the sexual harassment;  
* Such conduct is made, either explicitly or implicitly, a term or condition of employment; or  
* Submission to or rejection of such conduct is used as the basis for employment decisions affecting an individual’s employment.

## Examples of sexually harassing work environments include, but are not limited to:

### Physical conduct

* Unwelcome physical contact including patting, pinching, stroking, kissing, hugging, fondling, or inappropriate touching  
* Physical violence, including sexual assault  
* Physical contact, e.g. touching, pinching  
* The use of job-related threats or rewards to solicit sexual favors

### Verbal conduct

* Comments on a worker’s appearance, age, private life, etc.  
* Sexual comments, stories and jokes  
* Sexual advances  
* Repeated and unwanted social invitations for dates or physical intimacy  
* Insults based on the sex of the worker  
* Condescending or paternalistic remarks  
* Sending sexually explicit messages (by phone, email, or chat channels)

### Non-verbal conduct

* Display of sexually explicit or suggestive material  
* Sexually-suggestive gestures  
* Whistling  
* Leering  
* Unlawful sexual harassment is not limited to the physical workplace itself. It can occur while employees are traveling for business, at employer-sponsored events or parties, or over teleconferencing. Calls, texts, emails, and social media usage by employees can constitute unlawful workplace harassment, even if they occur away from the workplace premises, on personal devices or during non-work hours.

## Disputes & Dispute Resolution 

In a perfect world, employers and employees get along in every scenario. However, we know things can become disharmonious and disagreements can persist. Although we seek to provide a workplace in which all employees feel that they are an important part of the Company and where employees feel fairly treated, there may be times when you have a dispute with a supervisor or the Company which can best be resolved through a formal procedure for dispute resolution.

### Seek a Mediator and Arbitrator

All employees of Nymbl agree to first seek to mediate any dispute with the Company with a mediator from the American Arbitration Association or similar organization trained and experienced in employment disputes. If mediation is not successful, both the Company and the employee agree to submit their dispute to arbitration. The arbitrator will be chosen from a panel presented by the American Arbitration Association or another such organization that is acceptable to both parties. The cost of the arbitrator will be split between the Company and the employee. Each party will be responsible for its own attorney and other related fees. Both the Company and the employee acknowledge that by agreeing to arbitration, each gives up its right to litigate their employment dispute in court or to submit it to a jury. The decision of the arbitrator is final and binding.

### Seek a Court to Enforce Arbitration

However, either party may seek to have a court of competent jurisdiction enforce an arbitration award. In addition, the Company retains the right to seek injunctive or other relief in the case of misappropriation of trade secrets or other confidential information, or any other action by an employee which might reasonably be expected to lead to irreparable harm to the Company.

All disputes between any employee and the Company are to be resolved in accordance with the following procedure. Please note, however, that the Company reserves the right to modify this procedure at any time and nothing in this procedure should be construed to constitute a contract between you and the Company or to constitute any part of a contract between you and the Company.

Any dispute between you and the Company may be resolved using this grievance procedure, with the exception of oral reprimands which are not recorded in your personnel file.

A grievance is a complaint by an employee concerning any matter related to the employee’s employment with the Company. All grievances must be in writing. Using the form provided by the Company, you must state clearly and concisely all the known facts related to your grievance, including “who, what, where, when and the why.” Clearly explain why you disagree with the act or omission that forms the basis for the grievance. Also explain what remedy you are requesting. You must sign and date the grievance.

# Employee Resignation & Termination

This policy outlines the procedures for termination of employment, whether initiated by Nymbl or the employee, ensuring a smooth transition and adherence to all legal and organizational requirements.

## Employee Resignation

If an employee decides to resign, we request the following:

* **Notice Period**: A minimum of two weeks’ written notice is preferred to allow for adequate transition planning and knowledge transfer.  
* **Written Notification**: Submit a formal resignation letter to your manager and HR specifying your last working day.  
* **Transition Assistance**: Employees are encouraged to assist with the handover of responsibilities to ensure a smooth transition.

### Upon resignation

* **Final Pay**: The final paycheck, including any accrued but unused PTO (if applicable), will be processed in accordance with local labor laws.  
* **Exit Interview**: Employees may be invited to participate in an exit interview to provide feedback and insights about their experience at Nymbl.  
* **Return of Company Property**: All company property, including equipment, documents, and access credentials, must be returned before the final day of employment.

## Termination

Employment with Nymbl is at-will, meaning either the employee or the company may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause, and with or without notice, unless otherwise specified by applicable laws or agreements.

In cases where Nymbl initiates termination, the following steps will be observed:

* **Notice**: When possible, Nymbl will provide notice of termination, except in cases of immediate dismissal for cause (e.g., policy violations or misconduct).  
* **Final Pay**: The final paycheck, including payment for all accrued but unused PTO (if applicable), will be provided in compliance with local labor laws.  
* **Exit Process**: Employees will receive information on the status of benefits, final pay, and the return of company property.
